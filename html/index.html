Notes on the Observer

To name a thing is already to have changed it. The map is not the territory, but neither is the territory innocent of the map — once surveyed, it begins to remember being surveyed.

We mistake repetition for permanence. A wave returning to the shore is not the same wave; only the shape persists, and the shape is borrowed. What we call identity is the borrowing's habit.

The observer who looks for nothing finds the world. The observer who looks for something finds only the looking.

Every boundary is a question the universe is asking itself about where it ends. The answers are provisional and the questions outlive them.
